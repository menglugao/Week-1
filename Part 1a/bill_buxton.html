Bill Buxton

Company: Microsoft
Position: Principal Researcher

Bill Buxton is a computer scientist in the human-computer interaction field and a UX designer. He currently serves as the Principal Researcher at Microsoft Research. He holds a bachelor's degree in music from Queen's University and a master's degree in computer science from University of Toronto. Bill's achievements in the HCI field includes applying Fitts' law to human-computer interaction, the invention and analysis of the marking menu, and developing multi-touch interfaces and music composition tools. He is also the author of Sketching User Experiences: Getting the Design Right and the Right Design.
